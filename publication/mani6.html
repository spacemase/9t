Nicholas Davis - From The Half Clutch To The Crutch


No, it won’t be

The bejeweled tools

That set one free.

And it won’t be

Akin to any Luxury.

Because even when one sleeps,

Clutching the diamond-eyed teddy bears

And nearly extinct furs,

It is the dreams that occur.
(This is the way)
More so when one has only just awoken.

This moment of Lucid ignition.

Half-opened eyes widening with realization,

As the unconvicted, conveniently-lazied bombs

Explode.

Taking with them all the priceless crutches

On the perpetual pilgrimage

To the Matter Recycling Factory.

But with a frantic jolt,

It will all rewind

To the very first day.

Where once again
(And again)
One may bathe

In the Simpson and Seinfeld repeats,

Rinsing all the dream-colored waters out of the soul,

Sending them

Back to

From whence they came.
