Francesca Davidson - The Love You Take is Equal to the Love You Make


You can never really claim what is not of your own creation.

You may take it.
hold it.
hear it.
feel it.
But you can not know it.
understand it.
own it.

The light of the world is contained in a glass of prosecco.
Take it in your hand,
hold it up to the afternoon sun,
and see life in the amber crystals dancing in the bowl,

But until you take it in the mouth,
it can not exist for you.
Until your eyes swell and head sways
and the wine becomes you,
You can not know it.

The light of the world is in the pitch of a child's laughter.
Hear it in the hitch,
see it in the full-upper-mouth-grin,
the crooked baby teeth.

But unless you provoked it,
inspired it.
You can not understand it.

The light of the world resides along the edge of a collar bone,
of a woman in a shadowed room.
The moon glows upon her left shoulder
and the small concave place below her neck.
You may feel it in your longing,
see it in the soft beauty of the room,

But until you have rested your cheek there,
and felt the subtle movement it makes,
as she quietly gasps then exhales,
You can not own it.

In this world and any other,
all lack comes from that which is kept at a distance,
all that is had has simply been sought out.
