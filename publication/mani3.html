Austin Miller


I stole this manifesto from someone else and changed all the words. These things are all over the place. Who would really ever even care?

That would be awkward if he read this wouldn't it?

But now I wonder if in all my apathy, insensitivity, and lethargy have I ever had reason for a manifesto. Have I ever held an opinion so high that I must make it publically known? To create an idea so thought out that I could write directions for the accustomed populous? The answer; overtly no.

I am an individual in a sea of originality, I am the routine.

It began in school, when the teachers would say, "He isn't stupid, he just doesn’t try". I could have wrote your essay, but drawing was more fun.

Parents get mad, but don't they always?

Now I work a nine to five, or a four to midnight, and sometimes a twelve to eight. I've listened to the teachers, the family, and my friends and now I think I'm smarter because I find everything boring.

Drugs would probably help.

Then one day I made a discovery: an unopened can of beer! Right there in the refrigerator next to the apples. I don’t remember buying it, so I hope I didn't steal it. It's okay, the guy was probably an asshole anyway. That beer though....

Chances are I drink too much.

But damn it Bill W. this is my world and it is bland. So I think I will drink that beer. It's a comfort from that routine and it makes me feel better about all the individuals with ambition, ideas, and most importantly manifestos.

My name is Austin and I never feared plagiarism. Fuck you Lloyd Blankenship, I don't think this was a manifesto at all..
